Lagniappe

Lagniappe (pronounced: LAN-yap) is a Cajun French word meaning "a little extra". I believe that as mathematicians, we should find little ways to give back to and get involved in our communities (local and mathematical) because of the opportunities that have been given us. This page is dedicated to the ways that I am getting involved in and giving, a little extra back to my community.

Professional Service

Math ACT Prep Workshop (December 2024 - present)

Each month, I go to the public library in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, and help high school students study for the Math portion of the ACT. This was a program I started as a way to help high school students learn how to study and to help grad students get into volunteering in the community. (link here ebrpl.co/actmath)

Mental Health Ally

I have and will always be a mental health ally. As a mental health ally, I am available to anyone who needs someone to talk to or needs help managing their stress, anxiety, and depression.

Undergraduate Mentorships

As a graduate student, I have helped mentor undergraduate students on different projects. I have participated in the Vertically Integrated Research Courses. These courses strive to put graduate students with undergraduate students and work on a research project together. Some of these topics vary in the areas of research. I have primarily helped with research related to topology and data analysis. Here is a link to a project I worked on with undergraduates in the Spring of 2026 that was recognized in the LSU newspaper. LSU Reville: Softball data analysis project

Additionally, I have mentored undergraduate students in a Directed Reading Program. This program pairs an undergraduate student with a graduate student to give the undergraduate student and oppurtunity to learn different areas of math and research that they would not be exposed to in a classroom. It is like a reading course, but for graduate and undergraduate students.
